Current projects
1. Elementary cognitive correlates of human intelligence
Investigation of the facets and components of the concept of speed of information processing as a fundamental determinant of individual differences in human psychometric intelligence (current project supported by a grant from the 'Fonds zur Foerderung der wissenschaftlichen Forschung' - FWF - in Austria; project No P11196). Development and empirical evaluation of a battery of paper-and-pencil tests for speed of information processing (current project; see a).

 2. Physiological correlates of human intelligence
The spatio-temporal patterns of cortical activation during the performance of elementary cognitive tasks are studied in relation to the psychometric intelligence of the participants using the method of 'Event-Related Desynchronisation' (ERD) mapping (current project, see 1.).

3. Heritability of elementary cognitive processes
In cooperation with Alois Angleitner, Rainer Riemann (University Bielefeld) and Peter Borkenau (University of Halle) the heritability of the speed of information processing (as measured by elementary cognitive tests) is investigated in a twin study supported by the DFG (Deutsche Forschungs-Gemeinschaft).

4. Speed of Information Processing and Learning Ability
The relation of speed of information processing to the concept of learning ability is investigated in a cooperation with Juergen Guthke (University of Leipzig) in the framework of the university partnership Leipzig-Graz.

Former projects
 1. Biological Rhythms and Cognitive Performance
The influence of circadian and ultradian rhythms on performance in a variety of cognitive tasks was studied in a two-year project, taking into account also individual differences in circadian rhythms (i.e. the 'morningness-eveningness' dimension). (Supported by a grant from the 'Fonds zur Foerderung der wissenschaftlichen Forschung' - FWF - in Austria; 1991-1993).

2. Development and Evaluation of a New Test for Diagnostics in Traffic Psychology
Assesment of personality-related differences in performance, especially individual reactions to informational overload (supported by two grants from the 'Kuratorium fuer Verkehrssicherheit'; 1990-1994).

3. Questionnaire Response Latencies
The psychological meaning of response latencies to (computerized) personality questionnaire items was studied with respect to the reliability and validity of the questionnaire results. Can questionnaire response latencies provide information about the honesty (and, therefore, validity) of responding, or - conversely - indicate lying tendencies? (1991-1993)
